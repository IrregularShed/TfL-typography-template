Paying the Congestion Charge

You only need to pay once, no matter how many times you drive in and out of the Congestion Charge zone on the same day.

You can pay in advance or on the day of travel. If you don't pay by midnight on the charging day after you drove in the zone, you'll get a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN).

Not all drivers have to pay. Find out about discounts and exemptions.

Congestion Charge Auto Pay (CCAP) This automated payment system will record the number of charging days a vehicle travels within the charging zone each month and bill your debit or credit card each month. £10.50 CC Auto Pay

Pay in advance or by midnight on the day of travel

You can pay up to 90 days in advance. So, for example, you could pay for one day, a month or a year. Or you can pay after the journey on the day of travel. £11.50 Pay online

By midnight the following charging day

You can only pay the following day online or by phone. If you travel on a Friday you have until midnight on the following Monday to pay. £14 Pay online

CC Auto Pay

You can pay the Congestion Charge in a number of ways. It's quickest and easiest if you register for CC Auto Pay.

Congestion Charge Auto Pay is an automated payment system.

We'll automatically record the number of charging days a vehicle travels within the charging zone each month and bill your debit or credit card each month.

Benefits of Auto Pay

  • Never forget to pay the charge again
  • A reduced daily charge (£10.50)
  • Protection from receiving Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs) as long as your vehicle is registered and your Auto Pay account is active
  • Register up to five vehicles

Registration

  1. Register for a customer account first. If you already have a customer account, you'll still need to register for Auto Pay
  2. Once you have a customer account you can then register for Auto Pay:
  • Through your online account
  • Or call us on 0343 222 2222 (TfL call charges)
